🛡️ Insurance UK

Broke people skip insurance. Then they get burgled, drop their phone down the toilet, or lose everything in a fire — and there is nothing. Here is the cheapest way to get covered, what banks give you free, and the magic words that get claims paid.

💬 Tom's Story

"I was burgled. No contents insurance. Lost everything. My brother went mad when he lost his jacket — no insurance either. A friend got burgled WITH contents insurance. Old for brand new. Everything replaced. Same situation, completely different outcome. One piece of paper, a few quid a month. That is it."

Most people think insurance is for people who have money. It is actually most useful when you do not — because you cannot afford to replace anything if it goes wrong. A £50/year contents policy is the cheapest thing you can buy against losing everything you own.

✔️ What Insurance Do You Actually Need?

Not everything. But these ones matter.

  • 🏠Contents insurance — yes, even in a rented flat. Covers your stuff if you are burgled, there is a fire, or flooding damages your belongings. Your landlord's insurance covers the building — NOT your stuff. From around £3–£5/month on comparison sites. Non-negotiable if you own anything worth replacing.
  • 📱Phone insurance — if your phone is worth more than £200. Cracked screen, stolen, lost, water damage. Either through your network, a standalone policy, or — often better value — included in a bank account (see below). Read the excess before you buy. A £100 excess on a £300 phone is borderline pointless.
  • 💻Gadget insurance — TV, laptop, Fire Stick, games console. Often bundled with contents insurance as an add-on. Worth checking if your contents policy includes "accidental damage" — many do not by default. Add it. Usually a few extra pounds a month.
  • 🚗Car insurance — legally required. Third party minimum. If you drive a cheap car, third party fire and theft is usually enough. Fully comp is sometimes only a few pounds more — always compare. Never auto-renew without checking — loyalty costs you hundreds every year.
  • 🪖Life insurance — only if people depend on your income. Kids, a partner, a mortgage. If nobody relies on you financially, you do not need it yet. Start thinking about it when circumstances change.
  • 🏥Pet insurance — if you have a pet. One vet bill can be £1,000+. Monthly pet insurance is usually £15–£30. If you genuinely cannot afford a vet bill, you genuinely need this.

✨ The Magic Words — How to Get Claims Actually Paid

Tom's phone claim was rejected because he said the wrong thing. Here's what to say instead.

Insurance companies look for reasons to reject claims. The words you use matter enormously. "I was drunk and dropped it" = rejected. "The device was damaged in an unforeseeable incident" = paid. This is not fraud — it is knowing how to describe what happened accurately and professionally. Gemini or ChatGPT can write your claim for you if you tell it the facts.

💬 Phrases That Get Claims Paid

Instead of: "I dropped it" "The device was accidentally dropped resulting in damage to the screen and casing."
Instead of: "I lost it on a night out" "The item was lost in a public place on [date]. I reported the loss to police [crime reference if applicable] and to my network to block the IMEI."
Instead of: "Someone nicked it" "The item was stolen from my person/property on [date and location]. A police report was filed, crime reference: [number]."
Instead of: "It got wet" "The device was subjected to liquid damage due to [an unforeseen incident / accidental exposure] on [date]."
Instead of: "My house was broken into" "On [date], my property was subject to a forced entry. The following items were taken: [list]. A police report was filed, crime reference: [number]. Evidence of forced entry is available."

The rule: describe the event factually and professionally. Remove emotion. Remove anything that implies negligence on your part. Always have a crime reference number for theft.

🤖 Ask AI to Write Your Claim

Copy this prompt into Gemini or ChatGPT "I need to write an insurance claim. Here is what happened: [explain in plain English what occurred, date, circumstances, items lost or damaged]. Please write a formal, professional claim description that accurately describes the incident, avoids language that implies negligence, and includes all relevant details an insurer needs to process the claim."

Paste what it gives you directly into your claim form or read it out when calling. It works. This is what the magic words are — professional language that removes grounds for rejection.

⚠️ If your claim is rejected:
You have the right to complain to the Financial Ombudsman Service for free. Insurers know this and often settle before it gets there. Write a formal complaint letter first — use AI to write that too. Reference the FOS in the letter. Many rejections get overturned at this stage. financial-ombudsman.org.uk →
